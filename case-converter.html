Frequently Asked Questions

A case converter is a text formatting tool that changes the capitalization style of your text. It can transform text into formats like UPPERCASE, lowercase, Title Case, and many programming-friendly formats like camelCase or snake_case.
camelCase is widely used in programming, especially for variable names in JavaScript, Java, and other languages. Example: myVariableName. The first word is lowercase and each subsequent word starts with a capital letter.
snake_case uses underscores between words and all letters are typically lowercase. It's commonly used in Python, Ruby, and database column names. Example: my_variable_name.
