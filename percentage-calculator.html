Frequently Asked Questions

To calculate percentage increase: ((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) × 100. For example, if a price goes from 100 to 120, the increase is ((120-100)/100) × 100 = 20%.
The basic percentage formula is: Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100. For example, if you scored 45 out of 60, your percentage is (45/60) × 100 = 75%.
Use Mode 3 (Percentage Increase/Decrease). Enter the original price as "From" and the sale price as "To". The tool will show you the exact discount percentage.

X% of Y = (X/100) × Y

% Change = ((New-Old)/Old) × 100
